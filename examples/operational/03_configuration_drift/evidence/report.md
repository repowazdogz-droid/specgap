## Limitations

- SpecGap does not prove the spec is correct. It identifies likely semantic divergence zones and checks extracted constraints (via Z3 over the abstract sandbox model) for consistency or implication failure.
- Constraint extraction is rule-based and recognizes a fixed vocabulary of sandbox constraints. Unrecognized phrasing is silently not extracted.
- Fuzzy / paraphrase-aware extraction (`--extractor fuzzy`) is opt-in and rule-first; fuzzy-extracted constraints are flagged `requires_human_review` and are not authoritative. The default `rule` mode is fully deterministic and offline.
- The Z3 model is a small propositional abstraction of sandbox behavior, not a model of a real kernel or runtime.
- Counterexamples are illustrative behaviors, not exploit proofs.
- A clean report means *no divergence was detected*, not that the specification is correct.
